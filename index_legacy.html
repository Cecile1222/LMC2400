LMC2400: Intro to Media Studies

Fall 2025 | CRN 91638

Welcome to Introduction to Media Studies. This course serves as a gateway to understanding the history, theory, and cultural impact of media.

Class Descriptions and Objectives

  • Present a look into media and their effects, cultural impact, and historical to contemporary approaches in media study. Connect theorists and scholars to the media they have studied and the media we experience today. Equip students with a broad understanding of the historical development and cultural impact of diverse media.
  • Address the broad term "medium" and explore the question of what a medium is and how it impacts us.
  • Provide theoretical tools for critically analyzing media texts and building a versatile vocabulary for formal and cultural elements of screen-based media texts and other cultural artifacts.
  • Encourage applying media study issues and developments to expressive, artistic, computational, or design-based work.

Activities & Grading

Attendance (15 Points)

We won't be taking attendance; instead, I will be calling on people in a semi-random fashion to answer questions.

Grading Policy: If your name is called and you are not present, the absence will result in a 5 point reduction.

To avoid a deduction:

  1. Notify me via email before class if you have a planned absence (illness, personal emergency, etc.).
  2. In the case of an unexpected emergency, notify me as soon as possible afterward.
  3. Documentation is requested for extended or repeated absences.

Discussion Lead (15 Points)

For this activity, you are going to lead a discussion with the rest of the students individually by preparing a brief synopsis of what the reading is about.

  • The synopsis needs to have the main themes, information about the author, and questions that are likely to help in leading a group discussion.
  • During the first week of class, each student will be assigned as a discussion leader for the remainder of the term.
  • Format: In-class presentation (15-20 minutes). Slides are not required.
  • Due: The day of the reading.

Rubric

  • 13-15 Points: Shows full understanding of the reading materials and ability to critically identify and analyze main themes and concepts. Presentation is focused, clear, well-organized, and engaging. Utilizes a variety of types of media. Demonstrates original thought and introduces new ideas.
  • 9-12 Points: Demonstrates adequate understanding, providing a summary of major ideas. Some major points may be lacking. Asks questions that partially deepen the discussion. Minimal analysis and critical thinking; response is descriptive with little interpretive work.
  • 0 Points: Missing.

Discussion Participation (5 Points)

Participation is mandatory because it will enable you to explore concepts within the class while also engaging in discussions and sharing ideas with your peers.

Rubric

  • 5 Points: Actively participate in discussions throughout the classes.
  • 3 Points: Sometimes participate in discussions.
  • 0 Points: Never participate in Discussions.

Museum Visiting (5 Points)

Students are encouraged to visit a museum in person to select an artwork that will serve as the focus of your Art Critique Presentation assignment.

Rubric

  • 5 Points: Upload a photo of yourself with the artwork you selected at the museum (Visit in Person).
  • 3.5 Points: Upload a picture of the artwork you've selected from the museum's official website (Visit Online).

Assignments & Rubrics

Creative Reflection Presentation (15 Points)

Description: Students are required to conduct an in-depth analysis of their previous works (e.g., films, media art, performances) or a completed/ongoing design project by integrating their practical experiences with media theory.

Analysis Requirements:

  1. Explain your artistic or design body of work, including initial concepts and development process.
  2. Detail your conceptual framework, influences, and the reasoning behind creative or design decisions.
  3. Compare past insights with new perspectives gained through theory, research, or reflective practice.

Presentation Goals:

  1. Showcase the quality and originality of your work.
  2. Demonstrate the deep intellectual concepts underlying your creative or design decisions.
  3. Make clear connections between theory and practice.

Format:

  • Similar to an artist's pitch. Highlighting both creative and theoretical aspects.
  • Time Limit: 4 Minutes + 1 QA.
  • Due: Upload deck to Canvas by 8:00 PM the day before your presentation.

Rubric

  • 5 Points: Integrate practical experiences with media theory to analyze artistic body of work.
  • 5 Points: Compare past insights with new perspectives, showcasing evolution of understanding.
  • 5 Points: Structure presentation clearly to effectively communicate ideas and theoretical insights.
  • Late Policy: Deduction of 3 points for each day late. >3 days not accepted.

Decoding Art Presentation (20 Points)

Description: Visit a museum (online or in person) and select a piece of art to analyze from a media studies perspective. Engage deeply with the artwork, considering its media, medium, style, historical context, and the artist's background.

Analysis Points:

  1. Media and Medium: Describe type of media/medium and influence on viewer perception.
  2. Style and Historical Context: Examine style and context; discuss contribution to meaning.
  3. Artist Information: Background, influences, and how they shape the work.
  4. Theoretical Analysis: Apply media theories from class.
  5. Speculation: Speculate on how the artwork could inspire new directions in media practice.

Format:

  • Prepare a 7-minute presentation addressing at least three points above.
  • Include visuals of the artwork.
  • Be ready to engage the class in discussion.
  • Time Limit: 7 Minutes + 1 QA.
  • Due: Upload deck to Canvas on Presentation Day.

Rubric

  • 5 Points (Analysis): Thorough and insightful analysis integrating media, medium, style, context, etc.
  • 5 Points (Speculative Insights): Thoughtful speculation on future directions.
  • 5 Points (Clarity): Well-organized, clear, engaging, logical flow.
  • 5 Points (Sources): Effectively incorporates relevant sources and theories.
  • Late Policy: Deduction of 3 points for each day late. >3 days not accepted.

Final Paper: Speculative Media (25 Points)

Description: Students will work in groups of 4-6 to conduct a speculative media analysis. Focus on a specific media topic (cinema, gaming, television, XR, etc.).

Approaches:

  • In-depth analysis: Select a specific technological artifact and speculate on its future (1-10 years).
  • Overall analysis: Explore the broader media landscape and speculate on its evolution.

Analysis Framework:

  1. Technical Aspect (Form): Design, affordances, interactions.
  2. Organizational (Creators): Values and influence of creators.
  3. Cultural: Cultural context, embedded values, intended users/exclusion.
  4. Speculation: Project future (1-10 yrs) â€“ positive, negative, neutral.
  5. Use of Authors: Insights from at least 3 class authors + 1 additional.

Format:

  • Written essay: 6000-9000 words, PDF to Canvas.
  • Standard academic format (abstract, intro, body, conclusion).
  • MLA citation style.
  • Due: Dec 10, 08:00 P.M.

Rubric

  • 5 Points: Accurate citation (MLA) & consistent.
  • 3 Points: Standard academic format.
  • 3 Points: Analysis including technical (1), organizational (1), cultural (1).
  • 7 Points (Speculation): Thoughtful/creative speculation demonstrating critical thinking.
  • 7 Points (Analysis/Sources): Insightful analysis using 3+ class authors & 1 external.
  • Honor Code: Any breach results in 0.
  • Late Policy: Deduction of 3 points for each day late. >3 days not accepted.
